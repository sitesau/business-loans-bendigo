Choosing the structure

The page draws a useful comparison between a chattel mortgage and a finance lease. It says a chattel mortgage gives you ownership from day one, can allow GST to be claimed upfront, and may include a balloon payment. It says a finance lease leaves ownership with the lender during the term, charges GST on repayments, and lets you return or buy the asset at the end.

That choice is usually about control, tax timing and how long you plan to keep the asset. If the equipment will stay in the business for years, ownership can matter more. If you expect to refresh the asset sooner, end of term flexibility can matter more. Either way, the best answer usually depends on the accountant's view of the numbers and how the repayments fit monthly cash flow.

The safest way to start is to define the asset, check the repayment horizon and then compare the funding type against the actual job, not just the advertised rate.
